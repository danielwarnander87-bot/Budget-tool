Debt Payoff Plan

Total debt balance over time
Avalanche (highest APR first) Snowball (smallest balance first)
Avalanche pays minimums everywhere and throws every extra dollar at your highest-interest balance first — mathematically the cheapest route. Snowball targets your smallest balance first regardless of rate — usually costs a bit more in total interest, but clears accounts fast for momentum. Once any account hits $0, its minimum payment automatically rolls into the extra pool for the next one — that's why the payoff speeds up over time even without adding more money.
"Save to avoid red" is the minimum extra cushion you'd need sitting in the account at the start of the month to keep your balance at or above $0 the whole way through — it's calculated straight off your lowest point. Add that much (or more, for breathing room) and the red clears. Amber-tagged rows are ones you didn't give me a firm number or date for (the 3rd Affirm payment, the second gas fill-up, the exact rent day) — I made a reasonable guess. Fix them and everything downstream recalculates. Unchecking a row removes it from the math without deleting it, so you can test "what if I skip Amazon this cycle."
